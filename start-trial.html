How to use TechImpact.online without a signup

You can start in one click. Open a calculator page and type your numbers. There is no account wall on the free tools.

A good first path looks like this:

  1. Open the SLA Penalty Calculator if you care about service credits.
  2. Open the Downtime Cost Calculator if you care about revenue and people time.
  3. Open the RTO/RPO Impact Calculator if you are setting recovery targets.
  4. Use Allowed Downtime or Cost of Nines when you need the time budget behind an uptime %.

Optional PNG export is available on some results so you can paste a summary into a slide or ticket. You do not need Pro for that basic flow.

What free tools include today

Free means the core math tools that already ship on this site. You can run them as often as you like.

  • SLA Penalty Calculator — estimate service credits when uptime falls short of a target.
  • Website Downtime Cost Calculator — estimate revenue and productivity impact of an outage.
  • RTO/RPO Impact Calculator — turn recovery targets into a yearly impact estimate.
  • Allowed Downtime Calculator — convert uptime % into minutes/hours (and the reverse).
  • Cost of Extra Nines Calculator — compare two SLA targets and the budget “gap” between them.
  • Guides — plain-English articles on SLA tables, credits, and disaster recovery basics.

Free also includes simple result views and basic export where the page offers it. Community support means you can email feedback; we are a small team, so replies may take a few business days.

Honest Pro roadmap (not shipped yet)

Pro is a waitlist product. Price is still TBD. We list planned features so you know what might come later — not so we pretend they exist today.

  • Branded PDF reports — cleaner board-ready exports with your company name/logo.
  • Deeper analytics — optional risk views (for example Monte Carlo style ranges) when we have a solid model.
  • Industry benchmarks — optional starting assumptions for SaaS or e-commerce. Free already lets you type your own numbers.
  • Team sharing helpers — easier handoff to Slack/Teams or shared workspaces.
  • API access — programmatic use for internal tools. Free tools stay browser-only for now.

Plain promise: If a feature card says “planned,” it is not live. Join the waitlist only if those extras matter to you. Otherwise keep using Free.

Privacy: math stays in your browser

Calculator inputs are processed in your browser for the core tools. We do not need your revenue figures, outage minutes, or SLA percentages in our database for the free calculators to work.

If you email us, only what you put in the message leaves your device. If you join the waitlist, we store the contact details you submit so we can notify you about Pro. Read the full rules in our Privacy Policy.

Who should use which calculator

Pick the tool that matches the decision you need this week.

  • Vendor negotiation / credits: Start with the SLA Penalty Calculator and the guide How to file an SLA credit claim.
  • Business case for reliability spend: Use Downtime Cost so leaders see dollars, not only “the site was down.”
  • Backup and failover design: Use RTO/RPO Impact plus the RTO/RPO decision matrix.
  • Understanding an uptime promise: Use Allowed Downtime to turn 99.9% into real minutes.
  • Comparing two cloud plans: Use Cost of Extra Nines before you pay for another nine.

A simple 15-minute trial plan (still free)

If you want a structured try-out without signing up, use this checklist:

  1. Open Allowed Downtime. Enter your vendor’s uptime % and the correct month length. Write down the minute budget.
  2. Open Downtime Cost. Enter a recent outage length and a careful revenue-per-hour guess. Note the total.
  3. Open the SLA Penalty Calculator with the same uptime miss. Compare credit size to business loss — they are rarely equal.
  4. If you set recovery targets, finish with RTO/RPO Impact and skim the decision matrix.

After those four steps you usually know whether Free is enough for your team. The waitlist is optional.

Limits of these tools

The calculators are planning aids. They do not replace your contract, your lawyers, or your monitoring data. Currency fields and sample defaults are starting points — replace them with your numbers. If a result looks wrong, email us with inputs so we can check the formula page.

FAQ

Do I need an account to use the calculators?
No. Open any calculator page and start typing. The waitlist form is only for Pro updates.
Is Pro available to buy today?
Not yet. Pro is on a waitlist. Price and launch date are still TBD. Free tools are available now.
Will you store the numbers I type in a calculator?
Core calculator math runs in your browser. We do not need those inputs on our servers for Free tools to work. See the Privacy Policy for details.
What does “basic reporting” mean on Free?
On-page results and, where offered, a simple PNG summary. Branded PDF packs are planned for Pro.
Can I use these numbers in a contract dispute?
Use them as planning estimates. Your vendor contract and measured outage data decide credits. These tools help you prepare; they are not legal advice.
Where should I go if I only have five minutes?
Open Allowed Downtime with your SLA %, or Downtime Cost with last month’s outage length and a rough revenue-per-hour figure.
